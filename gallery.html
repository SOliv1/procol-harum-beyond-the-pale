Exotic Birds & Fruit

Exotic Birds and Fruit is the seventh full-length studio album by British progressive rock band Procol Harum. First released in 1974 in Argentina, the album was called "Pájaros Y Frutas Exóticas" (literally translated as "Birds and Exotic Fruit"). The artwork on the cover of the album is by Jakob Bogdani, a Hungarian artist noted for his paintings centring on exotic birds and fruit.

The Band Origins

In April 1967, Brooker began working as a singer-songwriter and formed Procol Harum with non-Paramounts Keith Reid (poet), Hammond organist Matthew Figuitarist Ray Royer and bassist David Knights. Guy Stevens, their original manager, named the band after Gus Dudgeon's Burmese cat. The cat's "cat fancy" name was Procul Harun, Procul being the breeder's prefix.
